The capacity of hydrogen to power fuel cells in zero-emission vehicles, the possibility of domestic production, and the quick filling and high efficiency of fuel cell electric vehicles all contribute to the interest in hydrogen as an alternative transportation fuel.
Fuel cells that utilise hydrogen can produce electricity through a chemical reaction as opposed to combustion, with the only byproducts being heat and water. There are numerous uses for it, including in homes, cars, and portable electricity.
There are a variety of Benefits from using Hydrogen Fuel cells.Hydrogen is a Clean Energy Source and there are almost no emissions.Fast charging times with long usage times.More efficient, powerful, and reduces carbon emissions when compared to fossil fuels.
Alongside the benefits there are a number of downsides to Hydrogen Fuel.Overall the cost of the Hydrogen and lack of reliability.Its highly flammable and the charging stations would need to be integrated into other stations.Storage and the possible fire hazard.
